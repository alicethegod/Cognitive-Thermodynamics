## <a name="english-version"></a>English Version

### Overview

This repository contains the official implementation for the paper "Cognitive Thermodynamics: A Statistical Mechanics Theory of Grounded Intelligence".

This work introduces a novel theoretical framework, Cognitive Thermodynamics, to explain the long-term evolutionary dynamics and failure modes of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems, especially those in closed environments (e.g., trained iteratively only on their own generated data).

The core of this repository is a definitive experiment that simulates a "model autophagy" process in a "growing network." It clearly demonstrates the complete dynamical process of a closed AI system undergoing an orderly "heat death" and eventual collapse across functional, semantic, and organizational levels. The results provide strong empirical evidence for the paper's central insight: the "Perfect Internalism" bias of the Smeta (optimizer).

**Figure:** The main result plot generated by the experiment, illustrating the "six-act play" of the system's collapse.

### Theoretical Background

This research is built upon a series of formal theories on grounded intelligence. The core idea is to model an intelligent agent's cognitive network as a non-equilibrium thermodynamic system, introducing the following key concepts:

- **Cognitive Phase Space:** A three-dimensional space defined by Grounding Cost (C_grounding), Structural Complexity (S_complexity), and Groundedness (G(c)) to describe the microstate of any internal concept.
    
- **Macroscopic Metrics:**
    
    - **System Temperature (ICE):** Measures the overall semantic confusion of the system.
        
    - **Organizational Health (SO):** Measures the clarity and orthogonality of the system's internal division of labor.
        
- **Cognitive Boltzmann Distribution:** A hypothesis regarding the relationship between a concept's "cognitive energy" and its probability of existing stably within the system.
    
- **Perfect Internalism:** The central finding of this research—that standard optimizers possess an intrinsic bias to maintain internal topological efficiency and organization at all costs, even if it leads to a complete semantic detachment from external reality.
    

For more theoretical details, please refer to our series of papers (Link to preprint, e.g., arXiv or Zenodo).

## <a name="版本"></a>中文版本

### 概述 (Overview)

本仓库包含了论文 《认知热力学：一种基于统计力学的接地智能理论》 的官方实现代码。

这项工作提出了一个全新的理论框架——“认知热力学”，用于解释人工智能（AI）系统，特别是那些在封闭环境中（例如，仅依赖自身生成的数据进行迭代训练）的长期动态演化和失效模式。

本仓库的核心是一个决定性实验，该实验通过模拟一个“增长网络”的“模型自噬”过程，清晰地展示了一个封闭AI系统从功能、语义到组织层面，经历有序“热寂”并最终崩溃的完整动力学过程。实验结果为论文的核心洞见——Smeta（优化器）的**“完美内部主义” (Perfect Internalism)** 偏见——提供了强有力的经验证据。

**图：** 实验最终生成的核心结果图，展示了系统崩溃的“六幕剧”。

### 理论背景 (Theoretical Background)

本研究建立在一系列关于接地智能的形式化理论之上。核心思想是将一个智能体的认知网络建模为一个非平衡态热力学系统，并引入了以下核心概念：

- **认知相空间 (Cognitive Phase Space):** 一个由接地成本 (C_grounding)、结构复杂度 (S_complexity) 和 接地性 (G(c)) 构成的三维空间，用于描述任何一个内部概念的微观状态。
    
- **宏观度量 (Macroscopic Metrics):**
    
    - **系统温度 (ICE):** 衡量系统整体的语义混淆程度。
        
    - **组织健康度 (SO):** 衡量系统内部分工的正交性与清晰度。
        
- **认知玻尔兹曼分布 (Cognitive Boltzmann Distribution):** 一个关于概念的“认知能量”与其在系统中稳定存在的概率之间关系的假设。
    
- **完美内部主义 (Perfect Internalism):** 本研究的核心发现，即标准优化器存在一种内在偏见，会不惜一切代价维持内部拓扑结构的效率和组织性，即使这会导致其语义与外部现实完全脱节。
    

更多理论细节，请参阅我们的系列论文（链接到预印本，例如arXiv或Zenodo）。
